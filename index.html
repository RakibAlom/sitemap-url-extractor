Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions? We've got answers.

A sitemap.xml is an XML file that lists all important URLs on a website. It helps search engines like Google discover and crawl pages more efficiently. Most websites have their sitemap at /sitemap.xml (e.g., example.com/sitemap.xml).

Large websites often use a "sitemap index" that contains links to multiple smaller sitemaps. For example, a news site might have separate sitemaps for articles, categories, and authors. Our tool automatically detects and crawls all these sub-sitemaps.

No, there's no limit! Our tool can handle sitemaps with thousands or even millions of URLs. Processing time depends on the number of sub-sitemaps and your internet connection, but we've optimized for handling large sitemaps efficiently.

Absolutely! All processing happens directly in your browser. We don't store, log, or transmit any of your sitemap data to our servers. Your data stays completely private and secure.

Some websites block cross-origin requests or require authentication. Our tool uses multiple CORS proxies as fallbacks, but some highly restricted sites may still not work. In such cases, you might need to download the sitemap manually.

We extract all available metadata including the URL location (loc), last modification date (lastmod), change frequency (changefreq), and priority. This data is included when you export to CSV format.
